### Words and Phrases


#### serendipity (serendipity)

_/ˌserənˈdɪpəti/_ [noun]

Meaning: the occurrence and development of events by chance in a happy or beneficial way

Examples:
- It was pure serendipity that we met at the coffee shop that day.
- The discovery of penicillin was a famous example of serendipity in science.

Origin: coined by Horace Walpole in 1754, from the Persian fairy tale 'The Three Princes of Serendip'

Synonyms: chance, luck, fortune, happenstance

#### ubiquitous (ubiquitous)

_/juːˈbɪkwɪtəs/_ [adjective]

Meaning: present, appearing, or found everywhere

Examples:
- Smartphones have become ubiquitous in modern society.
- Coffee shops are ubiquitous in this city.


Synonyms: omnipresent, pervasive, universal


---


#### juxtapose (juxtapose)

 [verb]

Meaning: to place or deal with close together for contrasting effect

Examples:
- The artist juxtaposed modern technology with traditional farming in the painting.

#### ameliorate (ameliorate)

_/əˈmiːliəreɪt/_ [verb]

Meaning: to make something bad or unsatisfactory better; to improve

Examples:
- The new policies were designed to ameliorate working conditions.
- We need to ameliorate the situation before it gets worse.

Origin: from Latin 'melior' meaning 'better'

Synonyms: improve, enhance, better, upgrade

Antonyms: worsen, deteriorate, aggravate
